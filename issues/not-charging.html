Issue Guide

Device not charging properly

If charging is slow, intermittent, or not detected at all, check the accessories and the port before assuming the battery has failed.

Charging Cable check Port check

Common signs

  • The battery percentage does not rise after being plugged in.
  • Charging only works with one cable or one angle.
  • The device gains power very slowly.
  • Charging starts and stops repeatedly.

Possible causes

  • A worn cable or weak charger is limiting power.
  • Dust or debris in the charging port is blocking a proper connection.
  • The device battery is deeply discharged and needs more time.
  • The charging port or battery may be physically damaged.

Step-by-step troubleshooting

  1. Try a different cable and charger. Use a simple wall charger rather than a laptop port to rule out low power delivery.
  2. Inspect the charging port carefully. Look for lint, bent parts, or looseness. Do not force anything into the port.
  3. Leave the device charging for 30 minutes. A very low battery can take time before the display or indicator changes.
  4. Avoid heavy use while charging. Let the device rest during the test so background activity does not hide slow charging.
  5. Note whether the battery still drops while plugged in. That is useful information if support needs to diagnose a failing battery or port.
  6. Stop if the port feels hot or loose. Repeated plugging can make hardware damage worse.

Important warning

Do not keep using a charger if the cable is frayed, the port is sparking, or the device becomes unusually hot while charging.

When to contact support

Contact BOOX support if multiple known-good chargers fail, the port is physically loose, or the battery percentage never rises after a full charging test.
