Issue Guide

Frozen screen or no response

Use this guide if the screen looks stuck, touch does nothing, or you are not sure whether you are seeing a real freeze or a normal E Ink artifact.

Frozen Touch Display

Common signs

  • The page image stays on screen and does not change.
  • Buttons or touch gestures do not respond.
  • The screen shows ghosting that looks like a freeze.
  • The issue started after sleep, a heavy app, or a low battery.

Possible causes

  • The display only needs a full refresh.
  • An app or background task has stopped responding.
  • The battery is low enough to cause unstable behavior.
  • The screen or touch layer may have suffered physical damage.

Step-by-step troubleshooting

  1. Try a full screen refresh first. Some E Ink artifacts can look like a freeze even when the device is still running.
  2. Wait one minute before forcing a restart. If the system is busy, it may recover on its own.
  3. Perform a long press on the power button. Hold for around 10 to 15 seconds, then give the device time to restart.
  4. Charge the device if the battery may be low. Low power can make the screen and touch behavior inconsistent.
  5. Check for physical signs of damage. Cracks, pressure marks, or spreading black areas point to screen damage rather than a software freeze.
  6. Test again after restart. If the screen keeps freezing in one app, the issue may be app-specific rather than system-wide.

Important warning

Do not press hard on the E Ink panel to “unstick” the screen. Pressure can permanently damage the display layer.

When to contact support

Contact BOOX support if the screen remains frozen after restart and charging, or if the panel shows cracks, black blotches, or pressure marks.
